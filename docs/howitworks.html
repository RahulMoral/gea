How It Works

Our step-by-step guide to selling your property.

We’ve broken the process down into 14 easy to understand steps, written in simple english so you can understand what happens when selling your property.

01

The Valuation Stage

We appreciate that you may work unsociable or long hours and know that you are busy. With this in mind, we offer flexible appointment times. If you would like a valuation in the evening – no problem! – we are not tied to the traditional 9-5 office hours. Just one of the things that we do differently.

02

After the Appointment

After the initial valuation, clients often like to think about it – no problem! – no pressure or sales pitch here, in fact there is never any obligation to use us whatsoever.

If you have any questions after the appointment – give us a call or drop us an email – we are here to make things easy for you.

03

You’d like to go ahead Fantastic!

When you decide that you would like to go ahead (we’ll be chuffed at this point) – we will arrange a second appointment at a convenient time to fill in the relevant paperwork. None of this is complicated and we will run through everything clearly with you.

This process usually takes approximately 1 hour.

04

For Sale Sign

For sale boards – these are a very valuable tool when it comes to selling as they generate enquiries. Around 70% of buyers will drive around the areas that they want to live in, therefore properties with boards will receive more enquiries. Ours are very distinctive too!

05

Energy Performance Certifcate is made

All home sellers are required by law to have an Energy Performance Certificate, or EPC as it is commonly called. There are a few exceptions to this – listed buildings being one of them. We will be able to advise you if you need one or not. If you do, we can arrange for this to be done on your behalf or you can arrange this yourself – no problem.

06

Brochure Approval

Once the brochure is prepared we will send it to you for your perusal. You will need to verify that it is fair and accurate representation of your home. Once approved we are able to distribute this to our client database.

07

Your property goes onto Rightmove

Rightmove is by far the biggest property web site in the country. It has incredible reach and power to generate viewings and enquiries for your property. As a company, we couldn’t be without it!

Your property will also be advertised on our own website.

08

In the newspapers

We will book advertisements in the local press on your behalf. Our adverts are designed to be eye catching, sleek and easy to read.

09

We put you up onto Social Media

We harness the incredible reach and power of social media to advertise your property.

10

The Viewings Begin

We firmly believe that the agent is the best person to conduct the viewings on your home. It is also advisable for you to be out when viewings take place (sorry!) If you are in at the time of the viewing, the viewer will have a tendency to just be polite and say nice things, which can actually be contrary to what they are really thinking. We would rather know exactly what they are thinking there and then. We can then use our expertise and experience to overcome any objections or sticking points. I’m sure that you will agree, you are far more likely to be open and say exactly what you think if you feel comfortable to do so.

11

We recieve and offer!

Once that the buyer is happy and confident that they would like to make an offer, they will either call or email us with an offer on your property. We would hope that the offer that they make is a reasonable one, however sometimes buyers can be a bit cheeky! We are experienced negotiators and are used to this, so don’t worry. We always act in your best interests to secure the absolute best price that we can.

12

Sale has been agreed

Once the sale is agreed you will need to find a solicitor. Sounds easy doesn’t it? - please choose carefully though. In our experience the service and communication skills of some have left many a client very frustrated. Always ask around for recommendations, including from us as we tend to know which solicitors are the best communicators – we speak to them regularly after all.

13

Sales Progression

The sales progression part of the transaction is often very overlooked, yet this is arguably one of the most important parts of the whole process. This involves regular contact and liaising with the buyers and sellers solicitors, yourself and your buyer. This often requires skill, patience and diplomacy, which we have learned over many years of practise. Problems can and do arise occasionally, but rest assured, you are in the best hands. We can resolve most issues that arise during the sales process.

14

Set the moving date

Once that your solicitor has everything that they need they will discuss exchange and completion dates with you. These are 2 separate milestones in the transaction and both are equally as important.

On exchange of contracts – the sale becomes legally binding and a deposit is put forward, generally 10%. Once that you have “exchanged” it is highly unlikely that anything will go wrong. At the point of exchange of contracts, the “move” or “completion” date is set.
